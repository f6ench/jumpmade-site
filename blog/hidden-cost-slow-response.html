Revenue Leaks

The Hidden Cost of Slow Lead Response

How responding 30 minutes late could be costing you 16,000+ per year.

Most tradespeople think they need more leads. They don't. They need to stop losing the leads they already get.

Here's the maths most people never run.

The revenue leak calculation

Take a typical plumber getting 20 leads per month with an average job value of 450.

If they respond in 30-60 minutes, data shows they'll win roughly 20% of those leads. That's 4 jobs per month, or 1,800 in revenue.

If they responded in under 60 seconds? They'd win closer to 78%. That's about 16 jobs from the same 20 leads. That's 7,200 per month.

The difference? 5,400 per month. 64,800 per year. From the exact same leads.

Even being conservative and cutting that in half, you're looking at 32,000 per year left on the table because of response time alone.

It gets worse: missed calls

40% of inbound calls go unanswered when you're on a job. That's not a character flaw -- you're literally doing the work. But every missed call is a potential customer who calls the next person on their list.

At 450 per job, missing just 5 calls per week costs 2,250 per week. That's 9,000 per month in potential revenue that never even enters your pipeline.

Then there's the follow-up problem

60% of quotes die from zero follow-up. You send a price, the customer says "let me think about it," and then... nothing. No follow-up at Day 1. No check-in at Day 3. No final nudge at Day 7.

The customer didn't say no. They just forgot. And you forgot to remind them. Multiply that across every quote you send and the number gets painful quickly.

The compound effect

Slow response, missed calls, and no follow-up don't just cost you individual jobs. They compound. The customer you lost today doesn't leave you a review. Which means the next customer is slightly less likely to enquire. Which means fewer leads next month. Which means less revenue. Which means you need to spend more on advertising to fill the gap.

It's a cycle, and it starts with response time.

What it costs to fix

A system that responds to every lead in 60 seconds, answers every missed call, and follows up on every quote costs 150 per month. One extra job -- just one -- covers the fee three times over.

The question isn't whether you can afford it. It's whether you can afford not to.
