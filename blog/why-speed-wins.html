Why the First Tradesperson to Respond Wins 78% of Jobs

The data behind speed-to-lead and what it means for your business.

When a homeowner needs a plumber, electrician, or builder, they don't send one enquiry and wait. They send three or four. Then they hire whoever gets back to them first.

This isn't a guess. Research across service industries consistently shows that the first responder wins 78% of the time. Not because they're cheaper. Not because they're better. Because they showed up.

What the data actually says

When a customer submits a lead on Checkatrade, MyBuilder, or any other platform, they're already ready to buy. They've searched, compared profiles, read reviews, and decided they need someone. The only question left is: who responds first?

The average tradesperson responds in 30-60 minutes. Some take hours. Some never respond at all. Meanwhile, their best competitor -- the one filling their diary every week -- responds in under 5 minutes.

The gap between "30 minutes" and "5 minutes" is the difference between a full diary and a quiet phone.

Why speed matters more than price

Homeowners aren't comparing three detailed quotes side by side. They're sitting at their kitchen table with a leaking tap, and the first person who replies feels like the right person. Speed creates trust. It says: "I'm professional, I'm available, I care about your problem."

By the time you respond 45 minutes later with a better price, they've already booked someone else. Your quote goes to voicemail.

The problem: you're on a job

You know this already. You're under a sink, up a ladder, or driving to the next job. You can't stop mid-pipe to answer your phone and type out a reply. So the lead sits. And by the time you get to it, someone faster has already won.

This isn't a discipline problem. It's a systems problem. You physically cannot respond in 60 seconds while doing the job you're paid to do.

The fix

The tradespeople winning the most work have a system that responds for them. Within 60 seconds of a lead coming in, the customer gets a message. The lead is qualified. A follow-up sequence is triggered. All before the tradesperson even knows the lead exists.

When they finish the job and check their phone, they don't see a missed opportunity. They see a notification: "New lead qualified. Tap accept or reject."

That's the difference between losing 3-6 jobs a month and winning them.
