What Your Competitors Are Doing Differently

The tradespeople winning the most work aren't better -- they're faster.

Look at the busiest tradesperson in your area. Full diary, picking and choosing jobs, turning work away. Now ask yourself: are they actually better at the trade than you?

Probably not. In most cases, the skill gap between you and your busiest competitor is negligible. The gap is in how they handle leads.

Same leads. Same trade. Different systems.

They respond faster

While you're finishing a job before checking your phone, they've already replied. Their customer got a response in 3 minutes. Yours is still waiting after 45. By the time you call back, the job is booked.

This happens multiple times per week. Over a year, it's dozens of jobs you never even knew you lost.

They follow up on every quote

You send a quote and wait. They send a quote, then check in the next day. Then again on Day 3. Then a final message on Day 7. Not pushy -- just professional. "Just checking if you had any questions about the quote."

That simple follow-up converts 20-30% of quotes that would otherwise go cold. Over a month, that's 2-4 extra jobs from quotes you would have won anyway if you'd just followed up.

They never miss a call

When you miss a call, the customer calls the next person. When your competitor misses a call, a voice receptionist picks up, qualifies the job, captures the details, and texts them a summary. The customer thinks they spoke to a real receptionist. Your competitor calls back within the hour with all the information they need.

Same missed call. Completely different outcome.

They collect reviews systematically

After every job, their system sends a review request. Not them personally -- they don't have to remember. The system does it at the right time, with the right message, every time.

After 6 months, they have 30 more reviews than you. After a year, 60. Every one of those reviews makes the next customer more likely to choose them over you. The gap widens.

It's not talent. It's systems.

Your competitor didn't become the busiest tradesperson in your area because they're a better plumber, electrician, or builder. They got there because they have systems that handle the business side while they do the work.

You can build the same systems. Or rather, you can have them built for you.

The question is whether you do it now, or keep watching jobs go to someone who isn't better than you -- just faster.
